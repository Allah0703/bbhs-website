Dengue and mosquito-borne disease awareness

Bangladesh and many tropical regions regularly face mosquito-borne disease risks. BBHS can use this space to share evidence-based awareness notes, prevention messages, event announcements, and links to official public health updates.

  • Share prevention messages in simple language for students and community members.
  • Link to official health agencies rather than spreading unverified numbers.
  • Prepare short educational posts on symptoms, prevention, and when to seek medical care.

Society news

This section can be used for verified BBHS activities only: launch announcements, meeting notices, training schedules, event reports, public health outreach activities, and member achievements.

For medical or public health updates, BBHS should link to reliable sources and avoid posting unverified case numbers or treatment advice.

Educational notes

BBHS can publish practical educational notes for students and young researchers. Suggested topics include how to search papers in PubMed, how to use Zotero or Mendeley, how to prepare a reference list, how to start basic Python or R coding, and how to use BLAST or QIIME 2 for beginner bioinformatics.

This content can gradually become a useful learning hub for Bangladeshi students and early-career researchers.

Open resource hub

Microbial Systems Journal

Microbial Systems is a scientific publication platform covering microbiology, microbial ecology, antimicrobial resistance, host–microbe interactions, environmental microbiology, and biomedical microbial research.

If formally approved, Microbial Systems may be linked with BBHS as an associated publication or scientific communication platform. The relationship should be clearly defined by the society committee before using terms such as “official journal” or “affiliated journal.”

Suggested current wording: “Microbial Systems may serve as an associated scientific publication and communication platform after formal approval by the BBHS committee.”
