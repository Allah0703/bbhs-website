Detailed focus

How each focus area can contribute

These descriptions explain why each focus area is included and how BBHS may develop activities around it.

Bioscience & Biotechnology

This area supports education and collaboration in biological sciences, biotechnology, molecular biology, genetics, cell biology, biochemistry, and related disciplines. It can help students and early-career researchers connect with mentors, laboratories, and training opportunities.

  • Seminars on modern bioscience methods and laboratory practices.
  • Workshops on data interpretation, experimental planning, and scientific presentation.
  • Networking among students, researchers, and biotechnology professionals.

Biomedical Research

Biomedical research is central to understanding disease mechanisms, diagnostics, therapeutic development, and translational science. BBHS can support discussion on experimental design, ethical research practice, and responsible interpretation of biomedical findings.

  • Training in scientific writing, peer review, and manuscript preparation.
  • Discussion on disease biology, diagnostics, therapeutics, and translational research.
  • Support for collaboration between laboratory scientists and healthcare professionals.

Microbiology & One Health

This area focuses on microbes, antimicrobial resistance, host–microbe interactions, environmental microbiology, food safety, and One Health. It is highly relevant for Bangladesh because human, animal, food, and environmental health are closely connected.

  • Activities on antimicrobial resistance awareness and microbiology education.
  • Scientific discussion on microbial ecology, infection biology, and environmental health.
  • Collaboration across human health, animal health, and environmental sectors.

Public Health

Public health activities help translate scientific evidence into awareness, prevention, and community-level action. BBHS can support evidence-based discussions on infectious diseases, nutrition, hygiene, environmental health, and health education.

  • Public health awareness materials and community education.
  • Training on reliable health information and misinformation prevention.
  • Discussion on health challenges relevant to Bangladesh and low-resource settings.

Training & Mentorship

Training and mentorship will support students, early-career researchers, and professionals who want to improve their scientific and career skills. This may include research planning, data presentation, grant writing, and academic career development.

  • Scientific writing and presentation workshops.
  • Mentorship for students and early-career researchers.
  • Career guidance, research ethics, and academic communication training.

Scientific Communication

Scientific communication helps make research understandable, responsible, and useful. BBHS can publish society news, educational materials, event reports, and public health messages while maintaining scientific accuracy and professional tone.

  • Newsletters, event summaries, and educational resources.
  • Science communication training for students and researchers.
  • Responsible sharing of health and research information for public awareness.
